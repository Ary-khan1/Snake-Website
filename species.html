Snake Species

Tree Snake

Habitat: Tropical Forests

The Tree Snake is an agile climber found in tropical forests. Known for its slender body and excellent camouflage, it hunts birds and small mammals in the canopy.

Desert Snake

Habitat: Arid Deserts

The Desert Snake thrives in arid environments with extreme temperatures. Its sandy coloration provides perfect camouflage among rocks and sand dunes.

Forest Snake

Habitat: Dense Woodlands

The Forest Snake inhabits dense woodland areas. This nocturnal hunter uses its heat-sensing abilities to locate prey in complete darkness.

King Cobra

Habitat: Tropical Forests

The world's longest venomous snake, reaching up to 18 feet. Known for its intelligence and distinctive hood display.

Python

Habitat: Rainforests & Grasslands

Non-venomous constrictors that can grow over 20 feet. They use heat-sensing pits to locate warm-blooded prey.

Rattlesnake

Habitat: Deserts & Rocky Areas

Venomous pit vipers with a distinctive rattle at the tail tip used as a warning signal to potential threats.

Black Mamba

Habitat: African Savannas

One of the fastest and most venomous snakes in the world, capable of moving at speeds up to 12 mph.

Anaconda

Habitat: Amazon Rainforest

The heaviest snake species, weighing up to 550 pounds. Excellent swimmers that hunt in rivers and swamps.

Coral Snake

Habitat: Tropical & Subtropical Regions

Highly venomous with distinctive red, yellow, and black bands. Shy and reclusive, rarely encountered by humans.
